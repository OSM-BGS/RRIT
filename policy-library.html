Section A: Regulatory & Policy Compliance

This section is concerned with ensuring that HR initiatives comply with Canadian labour laws, internal directives, procurement rules, and other relevant guidelines. The questions in this category prompt teams to identify all applicable legal and policy instruments before work begins.

Key Aspects Covered

  • Compliance with Canadian labour laws and public sector-specific guidelines
  • Adherence to privacy regulations and internal approval processes
  • Ensuring procurement and contracting approaches are compliant
  • Proactive measures for maintaining compliance with legal and regulatory frameworks

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments

  1. Canada Labour Code:
    Governs the rights and responsibilities of employers and employees. It establishes standards for fair treatment, including hours of work, wages, and leaves.
    Canada Labour Code – Justice Canada
  2. Policy on Privacy Protection:
    Guides the handling of personal information, ensuring it is managed according to legal requirements.
    Policy on Privacy Protection – Canada.ca
  3. Directive on Privacy Practices:
    Helps ensure that the privacy rights of individuals are protected in programs and activities involving the handling of personal information.
    Directive on Privacy Practices – Canada.ca
  4. Framework for the Management of Compliance:
    Emphasizes shared responsibility for understanding and following the rules that govern public resources.
    Framework for Compliance – Canada.ca

Section B: Data Security & Privacy

This section addresses the safeguards required to protect sensitive employee information against unauthorized access, in alignment with Canadian and international standards. It prompts teams to identify data sensitivity and initiate formal security and privacy reviews early.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • Data classification to identify sensitive and personal information
  • Initiation of formal Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) and Security Assessment & Authorization (SA&A)
  • Strong encryption for data at rest and in transit
  • Effective user authentication and access control measures

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Policy on Government Security:
    Ensures that government assets are protected and controlled. This policy covers the management of information security and physical security.
    Policy on Government Security – Canada.ca
  2. Directive on Security Management:
    Provides specific guidelines on how to manage security within government departments, including information security and personnel security measures.
    Directive on Security Management – Canada.ca
  3. Directive on Identity Management:
    Ensures effective identity management practices by outlining requirements for establishing, using, and validating identity information.
    Directive on Identity Management – Canada.ca

Section C: HR Technology / Integration

This section deals with evaluating the compatibility, integration challenges, and alignment of new HR technologies within existing systems. It prompts teams to consider both data inflows/outflows and future exit strategies to avoid vendor lock-in.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • Seamless integration with existing IT infrastructure using standard APIs or connectors
  • Data export capabilities to ensure data can be retrieved in a usable format
  • Alignment with the organization's digital strategy and enterprise architecture

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Policy on Service and Digital:
    This policy guides the digital transformation of government services, emphasizing digital-first approaches and the integration of digital tools.
    Policy on Service and Digital – Canada.ca
  2. Directive on Service and Digital:
    Provides guidelines on managing information technology and ensuring that IT solutions are effectively integrated within government operations.
    Directive on Service and Digital – Canada.ca
  3. Government of Canada Enterprise Architecture Framework:
    Provides criteria for reviewing digital initiatives to ensure they are well-architected and align with government-wide standards.
    Guideline on Enterprise Architecture Assessment – Canada.ca

Section D: User Adoption & Training

This section assesses the resources needed for a successful rollout, including training, user support, and communications. It prompts teams to plan for how users will learn the new system, get help, and receive important updates.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • Development of role-based training and quick-reference guides in both official languages
  • A clear and responsive support channel for users after launch
  • A simple communications plan to keep stakeholders informed

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Directive on the Management of Communications:
    This directive includes provisions for clear, effective, and accessible communication within government services.
    Directive on the Management of Communications – Canada.ca
  2. Official Languages Act:
    Ensures that the government respects bilingualism and supports the use of both English and French in government operations and services.
    Official Languages Act – Justice Canada
  3. Policy on Learning:
    This policy supports a culture of learning within the public service, ensuring employees have the skills and knowledge to perform their duties.
    Policy on Learning – Canada.ca

Section E: Cost–Benefit

This section evaluates the financial implications of HR initiatives, including initial and ongoing costs, funding sources, and the human resources required to operate the solution. It prompts teams to create a complete picture of the investment and its expected return.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • Analysis of one-time and recurring costs, benefits, and key performance measures
  • Confirmation of funding sources and accountability for ongoing costs
  • Assessment of the impact on employee time and what activities may need to stop to make room for the new initiative

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Policy on Financial Management:
    This policy provides the framework for sound financial management, including planning, budgeting, and reporting.
    Policy on Financial Management – Canada.ca
  2. Policy on the Planning and Management of Investments:
    Ensures that the Government of Canada has the necessary assets and services in place to support program delivery to Canadians.
    Policy on the Planning and Management of Investments – Canada.ca
  3. Financial Administration Act:
    Sets the legal framework for financial administration within the federal government, governing how funds are managed, spent, and accounted for.
    Financial Administration Act – Justice Canada

Key Aspects Covered:

Section H: Performance Metrics & Evaluation

This section evaluates how the success of the initiative will be measured. It prompts teams to define clear, measurable outcomes that are aligned with business goals and to establish a process for tracking progress against those goals.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • Selection of a short list (3-5) of outcome measures that align with business goals
  • Defining the starting point (baseline) and the target for each measure
  • Establishing a process for tracking progress against targets over time

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Policy on Results:
    Provides the framework for how Government of Canada programs define, measure, and report on their results to improve performance and accountability.
    Policy on Results – Canada.ca
  2. Directive on Results:
    This directive emphasizes achieving expected outcomes through efficient and effective resource use. It supports evaluating ROI and aligning initiatives with departmental results.
    Directive on Results – Canada.ca

Section I: Strategic Alignment

This section ensures the initiative supports broader organizational goals, has executive sponsorship, and does not duplicate existing systems. It prompts teams to confirm that the work is a recognized priority and has the leadership support needed to succeed.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • Pointing to a specific organizational priority or strategy the initiative advances
  • Securing a named executive sponsor who will champion the project
  • Verifying that no existing corporate system could meet the requirements

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Policy on the Planning and Management of Investments:
    Governs how departments plan and manage investments to ensure they align with strategic outcomes and represent value for money.
    Policy on the Planning and Management of Investments – Canada.ca
  2. Directive on the Management of Projects and Programmes:
    Provides guidance on project governance, including the critical role of executive sponsors in ensuring projects deliver on their expected outcomes.
    Directive on the Management of Projects and Programmes – Canada.ca

Section J: Accessibility & Inclusion

This section assesses whether the solution is accessible to all employees and designed with diverse needs in mind. It prompts teams to test with real users, including those who use assistive technologies, and to apply an intersectional lens (GBA Plus) to the initiative.

Key Aspects Covered:

  • End-to-end testing of key tasks by employees who use assistive technologies
  • Completion of a GBA Plus analysis to understand impacts on different groups of people
  • Use of plain, inclusive language in all content and user interfaces

Relevant Government of Canada Policies and Policy Instruments:

  1. Accessible Canada Act:
    The foundational legislation for creating a barrier-free Canada, which applies to all federal government departments and agencies.
    Accessible Canada Act – Justice Canada
  2. Standard on Web Accessibility:
    Provides the technical requirements to make websites and web applications accessible to people with disabilities.
    Standard on Web Accessibility – Canada.ca
  3. GBA Plus (Gender-based Analysis Plus):
    An analytical process used to assess how diverse groups of people may experience policies, programs, and initiatives.
    Introduction to GBA Plus – WAGE Canada
  4. Canada.ca Content Style Guide:
    Provides rules for writing clear, accessible, and inclusive content for Government of Canada digital services.
    Canada.ca Content Style Guide – Canada.ca
